# Marketing PDF Redesign System v2

## Design Goals

MARKETING-PDF-2 replaces the original dark brochure direction with a print-first, light editorial system.

MARKETING-PDF-3 promotes this approved direction to canonical public filenames:

- `/marketing/shift-evidence-product-brief.pdf`
- `/marketing/shift-evidence-product-brochure.pdf`
- `/marketing/migration-blueprint-overview.pdf`

The earlier versioned public filenames were QA artifacts and are no longer the primary public marketing URLs.

The v2 family should feel:

- Calm and premium.
- Technical-commercial rather than dashboard-like.
- Easy to print in an office environment.
- Easy to skim in PDF readers and email previews.
- Structured enough for MSP, executive and infrastructure conversations.

## Why Move Away From v1

The v1 direction was effective as a branded dark product artifact, but it had weaknesses for marketing use:

- Too ink-heavy for printing.
- Too visually dense for quick stakeholder sharing.
- Too close to a dark dashboard/report aesthetic.
- Less editorial whitespace than a premium brochure needs.
- Heavy panels made the content feel more operational than consultative.

v2 keeps the product truth and safe boundaries, but changes the visual language.

## Print-First Philosophy

The new system uses:

- Light ivory paper background.
- Charcoal body text.
- Restrained color accents.
- Low-ink cards and dividers.
- Larger effective whitespace.
- Repeated editorial rhythm instead of heavy full-bleed dark pages.

The goal is not to make the PDFs plain. The goal is to make them feel considered, printable and credible.

## Typography Direction

The generator stays within standard PDF-safe typefaces:

- Helvetica Bold for headings and labels.
- Helvetica Regular for body copy.

The design relies on hierarchy, spacing and line length instead of decorative fonts. This keeps text searchable/selectable and avoids broken glyph risk across PDF viewers.

## Color Palette Direction

Core palette:

- Paper: warm near-white.
- Text: charcoal and slate.
- Lines: light blue-gray.
- Accent cyan: product/evidence.
- Accent orange: VMware-to-Proxmox/Blueprint commercial framing.
- Accent green: readiness/trust.
- Accent violet: advisor/planning.

Color is used as an orientation system, not as a background flood.

## Layout And Grid Principles

v2 uses:

- Consistent page margins.
- Header brand lockup and refined footer.
- Section labels in the top-right.
- Two-column card rhythm where useful.
- Wider, calmer page openers.
- Tables/cards that hold short labels rather than dense paragraphs.

## Illustration And Diagram Philosophy

Diagrams are intentionally simple:

- Lightweight flow cards.
- Score bars instead of heavy dark radar blocks.
- Editorial comparison panels.
- No decorative chart noise unless it explains the decision model.

## CTA Philosophy

Brochure CTAs remain secondary.

The core funnel still prioritizes:

- Sample report.
- Demo workspace/replay.
- Pricing.
- Technical review.
- Sign-up.

PDF links support buyer education and stakeholder sharing; they should not overpower the primary conversion path.

## Accessibility And Readability Checklist

v2 should maintain:

- Searchable PDF text.
- High contrast on light backgrounds.
- No tiny dense paragraphs.
- Clear page labels and footer treatment.
- Explicit safety boundaries.
- Pricing truth aligned with current product configuration.
- No unsupported positive migration claims.

## How v2 Differs From v1

| Area | v1 | v2 |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Background | Dark, high-ink | Light, print-friendly |
| Visual tone | Product dashboard/report | Editorial brochure |
| Density | Heavier panels | More whitespace |
| Diagrams | Dark radar/blocks | Light flows, score bars, tables |
| Use case | Digital brand artifact | Owner-sendable and printable sales collateral |
| CTA posture | Product proof | Stakeholder education and next-step clarity |
